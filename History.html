1898

Malaria Defined

Sir Ronald Ross demonstrates that mosquitoes transmit malaria. He wins the 1902 Nobel Prize for this work.

1952

Global Malaria Eradication

Malaria is eliminated in the United States. Three years later, World Health Organization (WHO) launches Global Malaria Eradication Campaign, which excludes sub-Saharan Africa and is eventually abandoned.

1996

The Fight Against Malaria

Insecticide-treated bednets are proven to reduce overall childhood mortality by 20 percent in large, multi-country African study.

2016

Progress to Date

The New England Journal of Medicine publishes study with data showing that the malaria death rate in sub-Saharan Africa has declined by 57% since 2000. However, every year almost 500,000 children still die of malaria, a preventable and treatable disease.
